What is Oxalate in food about?

Oxalates in food are naturally occurring compounds found in many plants. They are a type of organic acid that can bind with minerals like calcium, forming calcium oxalate. This compound is not easily absorbed by the body and can accumulate in the kidneys, potentially leading to the formation of kidney stones.

Benefits of Low Oxalate Intake

Reduced Kidney Stone Risk

Lowering oxalate intake can prevent
the formation of calcium oxalate stones

Enhanced Mineral Absorption

Avoiding oxalates helps improve the
absorption of essential minerals like calcium, magnesium, and iron

Less Joint Pain and Inflammation:

Reducing oxalate consumption may
alleviate symptoms for those sensitive to oxalates
